# Financial Media AI Workflows

Use this reference when designing a financial self-media account, AI-assisted content workflow, content product, operating cadence, or compliance checklist.

## Core Thesis

AI makes financial content production cheaper, but it does not make trust cheaper. A durable financial media operation depends on reliable sources, repeatable thinking frameworks, user trust, compliance discipline, and a business loop.

## Representative Use Cases

- AIGC financial video production: turn verified market updates, investment-education topics, and standard explanations into narrated short videos, digital-human videos, or templated clips.
- Expert IP extension: use virtual avatars or voice only with authorization, disclosure, and review; keep judgment with the human expert.
- Topic radar: monitor policy, announcements, earnings, market movements, competitor accounts, user comments, and social search trends.
- AI-assisted research content: summarize filings, reports, and public data; generate drafts; require human verification and interpretation.
- Faceless finance content: suitable for business stories, financial literacy, fraud prevention, and historical explainers; high risk for sameness and factual errors.
- KOL and collaboration review: assess audience fit, content quality, brand fit, disclosure risk, and misleading investment-expression risk.
- Regulatory response: assume platforms and regulators may also use AI to detect misleading financial content; preserve sources, versions, review notes, and disclosures.

## End-To-End Workflow

1. Topic discovery
   - Monitor policy, announcements, financial reports, market movement, social keywords, peer accounts, and user comments.
   - Score topics by timeliness, audience value, source quality, differentiation, production cost, and compliance risk.

2. Information structuring
   - Summarize source texts.
   - Extract key numbers and named entities.
   - Create timelines, tables, and comparison points.
   - Mark claims that require source verification.

3. Research assistance
   - Break down revenue, profit, margins, expenses, cash flow, valuation, industry comparison, and risk factors.
   - Avoid buy/sell advice, target prices, certain predictions, and personalized investment advice.

4. Content production
   - Convert one verified source package into long article, short-video script, image-text post, podcast script, livestream outline, community brief, and FAQ.
   - Keep a human review step between interpretation and publication.

5. Video and visual production
   - Use AI for narration drafts, subtitles, covers, charts, storyboards, and templated edits.
   - Avoid unreviewed investment judgment and deceptive digital-human identity.

6. Community and private-domain operation
   - Cluster comments and DMs into recurring question types: losses, individual securities, macro policy, beginner education, fraud prevention, and investing psychology.
   - Turn recurring questions into future topics and FAQs.

7. Data review
   - Review title click-through, completion rate, save rate, comment sentiment, platform differences, topic lifecycle, and conversion path.
   - Output winning factors, weak-topic diagnoses, next-week topics, and template improvements.

8. Compliance first pass
   - Screen for guaranteed returns, principal protection implications, stock tips, target prices, undisclosed advertising, exaggerated headlines, rumors, and missing AI/digital-human disclosures.
   - Treat AI screening as support, never as final legal or compliance approval.

## Account Directions

- Hot market commentary: strong timeliness and clear AI efficiency gain; high risk if rumors become facts.
- Investment education: lower compliance pressure and good for long-term trust.
- Earnings and announcement explainer: useful when framed as information organization and analysis framework.
- AI podcast or audio finance: useful for daily recaps, weekly themes, research summaries, and subscription products.

## Operating Rhythm

```text
09:00 Generate pre-market topic pool
10:30 Human selects priority topics
11:00 Draft scripts and posts
12:00 Human reviews and publishes
15:30 Generate market-close recap
18:00 Analyze comments and performance data
21:00 Propose tomorrow's topics
```

## Moat Checklist

- Reliable sources: official disclosures, exchange data, reports, policy originals, historical data, and proprietary user questions.
- Thinking framework: stable way to interpret financial events, not generic summaries.
- Trust asset: long-term consistency, few factual mistakes, visible uncertainty, transparent interests, and traceable historical views.
- Compliance capability: rules, review workflow, version history, and responsibility ownership.
- Distribution and user operation: not only views, but fan quality, private-domain retention, paid conversion, repeat purchase, referral, and brand collaboration.

## Safe Language

- "This is a public-information summary, not investment advice."
- "AI-assisted draft, reviewed by a human before publication."
- "Figures should be verified against official disclosures."
- "Market risk exists; decisions should consider personal circumstances and risk tolerance."
